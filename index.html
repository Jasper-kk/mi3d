Abstract

A major challenge in collecting 3D data is capturing it under uncontrolled multi-illuminance in photon-limited scenes. Previous methods employed expensive LiDAR to mitigate illumination effects, often at the expense of real-world fidelity and model generalization. We propose MI3D, a novel benchmark for 3D indoor scene understanding under varying lighting conditions, capturing point clouds across three distinct and previously underexplored illuminance levels: 1–5 lux, 100–200 lux, and 400–500 lux. MI3D incorporates a dedicated Illuminance Control System (LCS) to standardize lighting conditions, enabling systematic evaluation of illumination effects on 3D data. It currently represents the largest indoor benchmark for point cloud classification and segmentation under multi-illuminance settings. The dataset utilizes three distinct illuminance levels and contains 17,366 RGB-D images and 8,683 point clouds. These were captured in 16 different scenes, showcasing a total of 73 distinct objects. Each scene was captured under three distinct illuminance conditions and four different height levels: two flat heights (1m and 1.5m) and two top heights (2m and 2.3m). Using this dataset, we have been able to train challenging state-of-the-art models tackling complex challenges. We believe that MI3D will advance 3D vision research.
